# Direct Competitive Set — SMB Word-of-Mouth / UGC / Loyalty / Referral / Ambassador SaaS
*Research date: 2026-06-12 · Method: 17 live web searches + page fetches (Shopify App Store, vendor pricing pages, G2/Capterra aggregators, pricing teardowns) · For: Social Perks reinvention inputs*

---

## 0. TL;DR market map

Social Perks ("perks for social-media actions") sits at the intersection of four established categories, each with its own incumbents, price bands, and distribution channels — and **the intersection itself (reward local/offline SMB customers for social posts, self-serve, <$100/mo) is essentially unoccupied**:

| Cluster | Who | Price band | Core loop | Serves local/offline SMBs? | Rewards social *posting*? |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Ecommerce loyalty/referral apps | Smile.io, Yotpo Loyalty, ReferralCandy, Loyoly | $0–$999/mo self-serve | points/referrals on purchases | No (Shopify-only) | Loyoly/Yotpo: partially |
| Reputation/review SMB SaaS | Birdeye, Podium, NiceJob | $75–$800/mo | SMS → Google review → local SEO | **Yes (their whole market)** | No (reviews only) |
| Ambassador/UGC platforms | Roster, Brandbassador, SocialLadder, BrandChamp, Social Snowball, Bounty | $249–$3,000/mo, often annual contracts | missions/affiliate links → content + sales | No (DTC ecommerce) | **Yes (core product)** |
| Influencer marketing suites | Aspire, GRIN, Later/Mavely | $20K–$30K+/yr | recruit paid creators | No | Yes (paid creators, not customers) |
| POS-native loyalty | Square Loyalty, Toast, TapMango, Thanx, Marsello | $49–$300/mo/location | points on POS purchases | **Yes** | No |

The graveyard (Belly, Perka, LevelUp, Lokalty, Gatsby's pivot, Carro's pivot) is almost entirely populated by companies that tried to build **two-sided local loyalty/advocacy networks without attaching to an existing workflow or distribution channel**.

## 1. Player-by-player profiles

### 1.1 Ecommerce loyalty & referral (self-serve, Shopify-centric)

#### Smile.io — the volume king of SMB loyalty
- **Pricing (verified from smile.io/pricing, June 2026):** Free ($0, 200 orders/mo) → Essential **$15/mo** (500 orders) → Standard **$79/mo** (1,000 orders) → Growth **$199/mo** (2,500 orders, then $20/100 extra orders) → Plus **$999/mo** (7,500 orders, $5/100 extra) → Enterprise custom ($40M+ revenue retailers). Month-to-month, no contracts, 14-day trials.
- **Traction:** **4,269 reviews, 4.9★** on the Shopify App Store; app live since **Dec 2014**. ([apps.shopify.com/smile-io](https://apps.shopify.com/smile-io))
- **Target/core loop:** Shopify stores; points + referrals + VIP tiers earned on purchases, redeemed as discounts.
- **What SMBs buy it for:** "loyalty in 10 minutes, no code" — retention insurance at impulse-purchase price points ($15–$79).
- **Moat:** Shopify App Store review flywheel + 11 years of brand. Distribution IS the moat.
- **Gap vs Social Perks:** purchases-only earning; social actions are limited to trivial "follow us" points; zero presence outside Shopify; no offline/local story; no proof-of-post verification.
- Sources: [smile.io/pricing](https://smile.io/pricing), [apps.shopify.com/smile-io](https://apps.shopify.com/smile-io)

#### Yotpo (Reviews + Loyalty) — mid-market suite in retreat
- **Pricing:** Reviews: Free (50 orders) / $79 Starter / $169 Pro / $799 Premium. Loyalty: Free (<100 orders/mo) / **$199/mo Pro** (≤500 orders) / $799+ Premium; **$0.20/order overage**; full suite ≈ **$368/mo** before overages; mostly sales-led, opaque list pricing. ([wiserreview.com/blog/yotpo-pricing](https://wiserreview.com/blog/yotpo-pricing/), [rivo.io/blog/yotpo-loyalty-pricing](https://www.rivo.io/blog/yotpo-loyalty-pricing))
- **Strategic signal:** In **Aug 2025 Yotpo killed its native Email/SMS products and laid off 200 people (34% of workforce)**, partnering with Attentive/Omnisend instead — the "all-in-one suite" thesis cracked even at their scale. ([wiserreview.com/blog/yotpo-pricing](https://wiserreview.com/blog/yotpo-pricing/))
- **Traction:** Yotpo Loyalty Shopify app: **944 reviews, 4.7★**, live since 2015. ([apps.shopify.com/swell](https://apps.shopify.com/swell))
- **Gap:** SMBs are actively priced out ($199 to get real loyalty features); the 2025 retrenchment creates churn and distrust in the segment beneath them.

#### ReferralCandy — the longevity proof for hybrid pricing
- **Pricing (verified on Shopify listing):** Basic **$39/mo + 10.5% success fee** → Grow **$79/mo + 3.5%** → Scale **$249/mo + 1.5%** → Enterprise **$799/mo + 0.25%**. 7-day trial.
- **Traction:** **1,454 reviews, 4.9★**, "Built for Shopify" badge, live since **Sept 2010**; claims 30,000+ brands. ([apps.shopify.com/referralcandy](https://apps.shopify.com/referralcandy))
- **What it proves:** SMBs accept **base subscription + % of referral-generated revenue** when attribution is automatic. 16 years in market on essentially one loop (give $10/get $10 referral links).
- **Gap:** referrals only — no UGC, no social proof-of-post, no offline.

#### Loyoly — the closest full-stack product analog (in ecommerce)
- **Pricing:** Free (40+ missions) / Lite **$99/mo** / Premium **$449/mo** (adds points for UGC + reviews) / Enterprise **$999/mo**; referral-only entry ~$37/mo. ([loyoly.io](https://www.loyoly.io/), [ai-cmo.net/tools/loyoly](https://ai-cmo.net/tools/loyoly))
- **Core loop:** loyalty + referral + **"missions"**: 40+ engagement mechanics including TikTok/Instagram tasks, reviews, surveys — i.e., *perks for marketing actions*, exactly Social Perks' thesis, executed inside the Shopify ecosystem with Klaviyo/Gorgias integrations and an Apple/Google wallet pass.
- **Traction:** 5.0★ on Shopify App Store, ~4.5 on G2; French origin, growing fast.
- **Gap:** Shopify-only, ecommerce-only; mission verification still largely manual/screenshot-based; $449/mo to unlock the UGC tier.

#### Social Snowball — post-purchase affiliate conversion
- **Pricing (verified on Shopify listing):** Snow Day **$249/mo + 3% of affiliate revenue**; Blizzard **$899/mo+**. ([apps.shopify.com/social-snowball](https://apps.shopify.com/social-snowball))
- **Traction:** **189 reviews, 4.3★**, live since Dec 2020 — strong DTC-Twitter brand, modest app-store footprint.
- **Core loop:** every checkout auto-creates an affiliate; "Safelinks" prevent coupon-code leaks; TikTok Shop + Klaviyo/Postscript integrations; instant payouts.
- **What SMBs buy:** automated word-of-mouth with cash commissions instead of points.
- **Gap:** $249 floor excludes true mom-and-pops; Shopify-only; affiliate-link mechanics don't fit walk-in local businesses.

### 1.2 Reputation/review SaaS (the incumbents who already own Social Perks' local SMB buyer)

#### Birdeye
- **Pricing:** from **~$299/mo**, sales-led; positioned for multi-location (100–10,000+ locations); G2 #1 in online reputation management 10 consecutive times. ([crazyegg.com/blog/podium-vs-birdeye](https://www.crazyegg.com/blog/podium-vs-birdeye/), [birdeye.com/blog/birdeye-vs-podium-comparison](https://birdeye.com/blog/birdeye-vs-podium-comparison/))
- **Distribution moat:** formal **channel/reseller program with wholesale pricing** for agencies, media companies and SaaS platforms. ([birdeye.com/partners/resellers](https://birdeye.com/partners/resellers/))
- **Core loop:** SMS/email review requests → Google reviews → local SEO lift. Now marketed as an "Agentic Marketing Platform."

#### Podium
- **Pricing (unpublished; consistently reported):** Core **~$399/mo**, Pro **~$599/mo**, Enterprise $999+; real-world single-location spend **$500–800/mo** after 10DLC fees, seats, SMS overages, and a $99/mo AI module. ([wiserreview.com/blog/podium-pricing](https://wiserreview.com/blog/podium-pricing/), [socialpilot.co/reviews/blogs/podium-pricing](https://www.socialpilot.co/reviews/blogs/podium-pricing))
- **Core loop:** SMS-first inbox + review requests; "text 2 hours after the appointment with a Google review link."
- **Signal:** local SMBs demonstrably pay $400–800/mo when the loop converts to reviews/leads — but resentment about hidden pricing/add-ons is a recurring theme in alternatives content.

#### NiceJob — the honest-pricing template for local SMB
- **Pricing:** Reviews **$75/mo flat**, Pro **$125/mo** (adds referrals + repeat-business automation + AI replies); Sites $99/mo + $199 setup with a 10% sales-lift guarantee; contract-free, 14-day no-card trial. ([get.nicejob.com/pricing](https://get.nicejob.com/pricing), [linktly.com NiceJob review](https://www.linktly.com/marketing-software/nicejob-review/))
- **Distribution moat:** native integrations with **Jobber, Housecall Pro, QuickBooks** — it rides the field-service-CRM rails of home-services SMBs (≤50 employees).
- **Gap (whole cluster):** these tools monetize *reviews*, not *social content*. None reward customers for Instagram/TikTok posts; none have a perk/wallet mechanic. They prove the buyer exists and pays $75–$600/mo — for a loop adjacent to Social Perks'.

### 1.3 Ambassador / customer-advocacy platforms (the core-loop twins, priced for mid-market)

#### Roster (formerly Wooly)
- **Pricing:** Starter **$599/mo**, Growth **$1,199/mo**, custom Pro/Enterprise; no payout fees. ([influencer-hero.com Roster alternatives](https://www.influencer-hero.com/blogs/roster-alternatives-features-pricing-reviews-for-effective-affiliate-marketing), [getroster.com](https://www.getroster.com/home/))
- **Core loop:** recruit ambassadors → social campaigns/actions → reward fulfillment + UGC collection. Customers: Specialized, Cotopaxi, Hydro Flask, On, Salomon.

#### Brandbassador
- **Pricing:** typically **$500–$3,000/mo**, commission-based + annual contract; add-ons $1K–5K onboarding, $200–1K/mo analytics. ([influencermarketinghub.com/brandbassador](https://influencermarketinghub.com/brandbassador/), [softwarefinder.com](https://softwarefinder.com/content-management-software/brandbassador))
- **Core loop:** "missions" (tasks across socials) → points/cash — the most direct conceptual twin of Social Perks' action library, sold at 10–60× Social Perks' price point to DTC brands with existing communities.

#### SocialLadder
- **Pricing:** starts **under $2,000/mo** + setup fee + seats + "challenge usage credits"; 6-month minimum; expects brands with 30K+ followers. DTC/campus ambassador focus (American Eagle, goPuff). ([socialladderapp.com blog](https://www.socialladderapp.com/blog/best-brand-ambassador-platforms/), [influencer-hero.com](https://www.influencer-hero.com/blogs/top-10-socialladder-alternatives-for-effective-influencer-marketing-pricing-reviews))

#### BrandChamp (acquired CrewFire, 2023)
- Custom pricing; ambassador activities → rewards; consolidation play — two of the mid-2010s "customer advocacy" startups are now one company. ([brandchamp.io/blog](https://brandchamp.io/blog/brandchamp-acquires-ambassador-marketing-software-crewfire/))

#### Bounty (bounty.co) — the consumer-side loop, executed
- **Model:** customers/creators of partner Shopify brands "get paid to post" on TikTok/Instagram; **paid per view**, content must stay live 15 days; brands set budgets **$1K–$100K** + monthly subscription + price-per-video; billed through Shopify. Recently tied to **gen.video** branding ("gen.video: Influencer (Bounty)" on the App Store). ([bounty.co](https://www.bounty.co/), [bounty.co/brands/faqs](https://www.bounty.co/brands/faqs), [apps.shopify.com/bounty-ugc-automation](https://apps.shopify.com/bounty-ugc-automation))
- **Why it matters:** proves consumers will post-for-pay at scale *when verification + payout are automatic*, and proves brands accept performance pricing (per-view). But it's DTC-only, view-count driven, and budget floors exclude mom-and-pops.

#### Micro-entrants proving the exact "perks for posts" loop on Shopify
- **Brandpay UGC Rewards & Loyalty** — "Meta (Instagram) approved" Shopify app: customers post Reels/Posts mentioning the business → tracked → rewarded per post or per engagement → gift cards at checkout. Tiny review base; budget option. ([apps.shopify.com/brandpay](https://apps.shopify.com/brandpay))
- **Storify – Story UGC + Rewards** — automated Instagram Story UGC rewards. ([apps.shopify.com/storify-sh](https://apps.shopify.com/storify-sh))
- Signal: the mechanic is being validated at micro-scale inside Shopify; **nobody is doing it for non-ecommerce local businesses**.

- **Aspire:** Essentials ~**$2,299/mo on 12-mo contract + $2,000 onboarding ≈ $29.6K year one**. ([partnrup.ai/grin-vs-aspire](https://partnrup.ai/grin-vs-aspire/), [stackinfluence pricing guide](https://stackinfluence.com/influencer-marketing-platform-pricing-2025/))

### 1.5 POS-native loyalty (the integration-moat owners for local)
- **Square Loyalty:** restructured 2025 — now **bundled into Square Plus at $49/mo/location**, no longer a standalone add-on; visit/amount/item-based stars + VIP tiers; **only works inside the Square ecosystem**. ([passtastic.io Square review](https://passtastic.io/en/blog/square-loyalty-program-review), [squareup.com/us/en/software/loyalty](https://squareup.com/us/en/software/loyalty))
- **Marsello:** loyalty + email/SMS across **Shopify POS, Lightspeed Retail/Restaurant, Cin7, Heartland** — the multi-POS loyalty play; plan-based pricing (Loyalty Launch / Accelerate / Enterprise), 14-day trial. ([marsello.com/pricing](https://www.marsello.com/pricing), [marsello.com/integrations](https://www.marsello.com/integrations))
- **TapMango / Thanx:** custom-priced branded loyalty (tablets, SMS, subscriptions-as-loyalty in 2025); Thanx is card-linked (auto-reward on payment, no sign-in). ([tapmango.com](https://www.tapmango.com/), [cuspera Thanx](https://www.cuspera.com/products/thanx-software-x-6906))
- **Nift:** performance gift network (15K+ businesses, claims 100M+ consumers); businesses pay **cost-per-person only when a consumer selects their gift**; distributes through partners (Constant Contact, Mindbody, ParkMobile). Pure pay-for-outcome local customer acquisition. ([gonift.com/business/faq-for-business](https://www.gonift.com/business/faq-for-business/), [gonift.com/business/how-it-works](https://www.gonift.com/business/how-it-works/))
- **Gap (whole cluster):** all reward *transactions*, none reward *advocacy/content*. The POS players own the redemption rail Social Perks lacks; Social Perks owns the social-action rail they lack.

### 1.6 The graveyard & the pivots (entrant-killer evidence)
- **Belly** (Chicago, 2011; $12.1M raised; 1,400 merchants + 200K users by 2012) — assets sold to Mobivity via Hatch in **2018**. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Belly_(loyalty_program)), [Fast Company](https://www.fastcompany.com/3016461/belly-raises-121-million-to-replace-loyalty-cards-with-its-app))
- **Perka** ($35/mo punch-card app, 400+ locations) — absorbed by First Data; gone as a brand. **LevelUp** (3,800 locations, monetized via loyalty campaigns) — acquired by Grubhub, shut down. **Lokalty** — shut, citing "the local rewards space is crowded." ([Entrepreneur/Technical.ly coverage](https://www.entrepreneur.com/growing-a-business/4-apps-for-increasing-customer-loyalty/224853))
- **Gatsby** — *the most instructive pivot*: started as exactly "reward customers for Instagram UGC," now sells "Convert Your Instagram Followers into Email & SMS Subscribers" at **$300+/mo** (120 reviews, 5.0★) and feeds mention-events INTO Smile/LoyaltyLion/Yotpo as points triggers. Standalone "reward-for-post" became a *feature of loyalty platforms*, not a company. ([apps.shopify.com/gatsby](https://apps.shopify.com/gatsby), [gatsby.ai/pricing](https://gatsby.ai/pricing), [LoyaltyLion integration manual](https://help.loyaltylion.com/en/articles/6291028-gatsby-ai-integration-manual))
- **Carro** — pivoted from influencer tools to cross-store dropshipping; installs **−50.4% YoY** (6,037 stores). ([storeleads.app Carro report](https://storeleads.app/reports/shopify/app/carro))
- **Yotpo 2025**: 34% layoffs + product-line amputation — suite sprawl kills margins even at unicorn scale (an external echo of Social Perks' own audit finding #3).

### 1.7 Platform-risk reality check (verification feasibility)
- Meta **shut the Instagram Basic Display API on Dec 4, 2024**; third-party consumer apps (Tinder, Hinge, Day One) lost Instagram access. ([TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2024/12/06/instagram-locks-out-developers-of-third-party-consumer-apps/))
- Mention/tag/UGC tracking now **requires the Instagram Graph API with a Business/Creator account and Meta app review**; hashtag tracking capped at 30 hashtags per 7 days. 2025 brought further crackdowns on non-approved tools. ([developers.facebook.com](https://developers.facebook.com/docs/instagram-platform/overview/), [postly.ai](https://blog.postly.ai/why-instagram-is-cracking-down-on-third-party-apps-in-2025/), [nosto.com/blog/instagram-api-limit](https://www.nosto.com/blog/instagram-api-limit/))
- Implication: **real post verification is a gated capability — and therefore a moat for whoever earns Meta approval** (Brandpay markets "Meta approved" as its headline). Social Perks' current stubbed verifier (fixed 0.95 confidence, per the 2026-06-02 audit) is the single biggest product-credibility gap relative to this market.

## 2. The four questions

### Q1. Where is the whitespace Social Perks can own?
1. **"Perks for posts" for local/offline SMBs — the empty quadrant.** Review SaaS (Birdeye/Podium/NiceJob) owns the local buyer but only does reviews. POS loyalty (Square/Toast/TapMango) owns redemption but only rewards purchases. Ambassador platforms (Roster/Brandbassador) do reward content but start at $500–600/mo, require annual contracts, and assume DTC ecommerce. The Shopify micro-entrants (Brandpay, Storify, Loyoly missions) prove the mechanic but are ecommerce-locked. **Nobody sells "your café/yoga-studio/vet customers post a Reel tagging you, you verify, they get a perk at the counter" self-serve at $29–79/mo.** Social Perks' only fully-built path (4-field signup → wizard → `/c/[id]` QR claim page) is already shaped for exactly this quadrant.
2. **Verified-post rewards as the differentiator** — earned via Instagram Graph API approval (business connects its own IG Business account, the API legitimately surfaces mentions/tags of that account). Gatsby and Brandpay show it's possible; nobody has packaged it for non-Shopify local businesses.
3. **Honest flat pricing as a wedge against Podium/Birdeye resentment** — the alternatives-content economy around "Podium hidden fees" is large; NiceJob built a durable business on "$75 flat, no contract." A $29–49 "turn customers into marketers" tool is a different SKU than their $400–800 comms suites, not a head-on fight.
4. **Where NOT to go:** Shopify loyalty (Smile at $15/mo with 4,269 reviews is unassailable), influencer marketplaces (Aspire/GRIN/Later own it; Influence.co shows the stagnation path), and any consumer-destination app (the Belly graveyard).

### Q2. What pricing does the market bear for SMB advocacy tools?
- **Self-serve floor:** $15–$99/mo (Smile Essential $15, ReferralCandy $39, Square Loyalty $49/location, NiceJob $75, Loyoly Lite $99). This is the no-sales-call impulse band. **Social Perks' Free/$29/$49 sits correctly inside it** — if anything, $49 Pro is below the demonstrated ceiling.
- **Proven mid band for local SMBs:** $75–$299/mo flat when ROI is legible (reviews→SEO, referrals→sales). $300–800/mo is attainable (Podium, Birdeye) but only with sales-led motion + SMS infrastructure.
- **Hybrid base + success fee is normalized:** ReferralCandy ($39 + 10.5% → $799 + 0.25%), Social Snowball ($249 + 3%), Bounty (per-view), Nift (pure CPP). A future "$29 + small % of attributed redemption value" tier is a proven upgrade path that monetizes success without raising the entry price.
- **Ambassador-platform pricing ($500–$3,000/mo + annual contracts) does NOT transfer** to mom-and-pop; it exists because those vendors sell managed programs with CSMs.

### Q3. Which distribution channels do winners use?
1. **App-store flywheels** — Smile (4,269 reviews since 2014), ReferralCandy (1,454, "Built for Shopify"), Yotpo (944). The review count *is* the customer-acquisition engine. Social Perks has no app-store surface today — its programmatic SEO is the only owned channel; a **Square App Marketplace / Clover App Market / Toast partner listing** would be the local-SMB equivalent of the Shopify App Store.
2. **Riding vertical-SaaS rails** — NiceJob via Jobber/Housecall Pro/QuickBooks; Marsello via Lightspeed/Cin7/Shopify POS; Nift embedded in Constant Contact/Mindbody/ParkMobile. Winners attach to software the SMB already opens daily.
3. **Agency/reseller channels** — Birdeye runs a formal wholesale reseller program; Grade.us ($180/mo) and TrueReview win agencies with white-label + per-client billing. Local-marketing agencies are a repeatable multiplier for review/advocacy tools.
4. **Outbound sales-led SMS demos** (Podium) — works but needs venture-scale CAC budgets; not available to Social Perks pre-revenue.
5. **Consumer-side network effects** (Bounty's creator pool, Mavely's 120K creators → $250M exit) — powerful but capital-hungry; a cold-start trap for a pre-launch product.

### Q4. What kills entrants in this space?
1. **Two-sided cold start in local advocacy** — Belly, Perka, LevelUp, Lokalty all needed consumers to adopt an app/network and merchants to join simultaneously; all are dead or absorbed. Survivors attached to one side's existing rail (POS, CRM, app store). *Social Perks' QR-claim page (no consumer app) is the right instinct; a consumer destination would be the wrong one.*
2. **Platform API dependence without approval** — the Dec 2024 Basic Display shutdown and 2025 Meta crackdowns mean unverified "post for perks" degrades to screenshots-and-honor-system → fraud → businesses stop paying. Entrants who never secure Graph API/app-review die slowly; the audit's fixed-0.95-confidence verification stub is precisely this failure mode in embryo.
3. **SMB churn economics** — 3–7% monthly churn (31–58%/yr), with **43% of losses in the first 90 days** ([optif.ai](https://optif.ai/learn/questions/b2b-saas-churn-rate-benchmark/), [culta.ai](https://culta.ai/blog/saas-churn-rate-guide-benchmarks)). At $29–49/mo that caps LTV near $400–700 — sales-led CAC is fatal; onboarding must demonstrate a posted-and-redeemed perk within days (the auto-launching wizard matters more than any feature).
4. **Feature absorption by adjacent incumbents** — Gatsby's reward-for-UGC loop became an integration feeding Smile/Yotpo points; Square bundled loyalty into its $49 Plus plan; Yotpo/Smile can add "Instagram mission" points anytime. A standalone tool must own something incumbents can't trivially copy (verification approval, the local redemption UX, or an SMB-priced action library).
5. **Reward/affiliate fraud** — coupon-code leaking (Social Snowball's Safelinks exist because of it), post-then-delete (Bounty's 15-day retention rule), fake engagement. Fraud controls are table stakes the market has already learned to demand.
6. **Suite sprawl before product-market fit** — Yotpo's 34% layoff and product amputations at unicorn scale; Carro's −50% installs after pivoting; this is the market-level version of Social Perks' own audit finding (#3 sprawl). Single-loop tools (ReferralCandy, 16 years; NiceJob) outlive suites that scale before focus.
7. **Market tailwind worth noting (not a killer — a timer):** influencer spend hit **$32.55B in 2026 with 45.5% flowing to nano/micro creators**; 73% of brands prefer micro/nano; nano posts price under $500 ([influencermarketinghub benchmark](https://influencermarketinghub.com/influencer-marketing-benchmark-report/), [digitalapplied.com](https://www.digitalapplied.com/blog/influencer-marketing-2026-micro-nano-strategy)). The "everyday customer as nano-creator" wave is cresting now — whitespace doesn't stay empty.

---

## 3. Implications for Social Perks (synthesis)
1. **Double down on the local/offline wedge** — it is the only quadrant with a paying buyer (proven by NiceJob/Podium/Square) and no "perks for posts" incumbent. Deprioritize anything that competes with Shopify-ecosystem loyalty.
2. **Real Instagram Graph API verification is the moat to build** (and the current stub is the biggest gap vs. Brandpay/Gatsby/Bounty). "Meta-approved verification" is a marketable badge in this category.
3. **Pricing is roughly right; add a success-fee growth path** — keep Free/$29/$49 for entry; the market tolerates "base + % of attributed value" as the upsell (ReferralCandy/Social Snowball pattern).
4. **Distribution must be borrowed, not built**: Square/Clover/Toast marketplace listings and local-marketing-agency white-label are the two channels with precedent; programmatic SEO is a legitimate third leg already in place.
5. **Speed-to-first-redemption is the retention game**: 43% of SMB churn happens in 90 days; the existing 3-step auto-launching wizard + QR claim page is the right asset — instrument it relentlessly.
